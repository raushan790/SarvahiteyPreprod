Let's help ONE

Let's help ALL


We at Sarvahitey not only believe in this, but we take it upon ourselves, that each of our endeavor should be such that these words find their true meaning; that our efforts should be such that no fellow human being remains cloth-less or hungry or suffering.

Gradually, step by step, we're initiating and pursuing various projects through which we, those who have, are able to reach out to more and more people, those who don't. We are continuously trying to bridge the gap between those who have been fortunate and those who have been less fortunate.

In this mission, after conducting many successful clothes donation drives, teaching the underprivileged and doing relief work, all of which we shall keep continuing, our latest endeavor is conducting FOOD DRIVES.

We are strong proposers of the social way of living. An equitable distribution of resources should be the main aim of a State, as we believe. However, there are some who are unable to afford the resources which others can. In fact, those who can, do not at times, appreciate the importance of such resources. A meal at a fine-dine for some is a weekly event, while for others, three full meals a day is a dream.

Location: India


Keeping this in mind, the volunteers of Sarvahitey collected donations from people who were capable enough, and decided to distribute it among those who weren’t as lucky. However, as we do not distribute money owing to its unnatural use among the blue-collared, we decided to present such blue-collared gentry of Noida with some food, as lunch, and some food for thought.

After an insightful and igniting meeting, members of Sarvahitey distributed food at Sector 18, Noida as part of the new initiative, 'Food Drive'. More than 200 packets of food were distributed in a span of 25-30 minutes. Each packet comprised of 4 pooris, aaloo ki sabji, and sooji ka halwa (cooked in desi ghee), all of which was monitored by our volunteers. The volunteers distributed such pre-packed packets to rickshaw-pullers, construction workers, beggars, homeless people, security guards and other such labourers. As we believe in cleanliness as much as any other social issue, all those who were given food were instructed to throw the plastic bags in the dustbin; almost everyone followed. The children who asked for alms in their daily routine, asked those who we served for packets to throw in the dustbin.

The pleasure and satisfaction of feeding even one hungry person is unparalleled, and we fed more than 200 people. Our rewards were twinkles in the eyes of kids while getting food, and smiles on their faces after finishing their meal. Special thanks to all those who sponsored this food drive, without you this would not have been possible

Lastly, we cannot but mention that it was a learning experience for all the members of Sarvahitey. Moreover, we got some ideas, which we hope to successfully implement in the next food drive.
